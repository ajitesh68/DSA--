55. Jump Game              A+
The thought process is to iterate through each position, and if the current index i is reachable, 
"unlock" all subsequent indices that fall within the jump range (nums[i]) by marking them true. 
If the loop encounters an index that was never unlocked, it returns false; otherwise, it returns 
true as soon as the last index is marked reachable.


45. Jump Game II           A+   (45 min)
Think of the problem not as jumping from index to index, but as pushing a "window" of reachability
forward. As you iterate through the current window (defined by current_end), you constantly calculate 
the max potential reach (farthest) for the next window. You delay incrementing the jumps counter as 
long as possible—only when you hit the end of your current window (i == current_end) do you 
acknowledge that a new jump is strictly necessary to proceed, at which point you update your 
window to the farthest point you found


376. Wiggle Subsequence           A+(1 hr  15 min)
Think of this as hiking across a mountain range where you want to plant a flag at every **Peak** (top) and **Valley** (bottom).

* **Climbing Up:** If you are going up and find a point higher than where you are, you don't stop. 
You move to that higher point (`update new_peak`) because being higher makes the drop deeper later.

* **Planting the Flag:** You only count a step (`seq_size++`) when the path **changes direction** 
(e.g., you were going up, but now you must go down).

We greedily chase the most extreme values to maximize our chances of wiggling back the other way.

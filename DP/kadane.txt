1186. Maximum Subarray Sum with One Deletion                       B  -> A

Thought Process: Parallel Timelines
Imagine you are walking through the array in two parallel timelines simultaneously:

Timeline A (no_del): You play by standard rules (Kadane's). You never delete anything.

Timeline B (one_del): You have already used your "delete" power. At every new number, Timeline B updates by making a choice: "Do I extend my current path
(keep the old deletion), or do I jump over from Timeline A by deleting this specific number?" You simply track the maximum of these two timelines at every step.


This pattern is called State Machine DP or DP with States.

This can also be done by recursion + memoization





978. Longest Turbulent Subarray                                   B 
### Thought Process (State Machine DP)

The core insight is **State Dependency**. Since a turbulent subarray requires alternating directions (), the validity of the *current* step depends entirely on the 
*previous* step's direction. We cannot use a single counter; we must simultaneously track two independent timelines:

1. **`up`**: The length of a valid run ending with an increase.
2. **`down`**: The length of a valid run ending with a decrease.

At each step, an "increase" extends an existing "decrease" chain, and vice versa.

### What You Were Missing (Gap Analysis)

* **Equality Handling:** Your logic treated `arr[i] == arr[i-1]` as a "break" but failed to explicitly reset the counters to 1.
* **Sequential Logic Flaw:** You used sequential `if` statements (`if(up==down)... if(up>down)`). This caused a "double update" where one state changed, and the very 
next line incorrectly processed that *new* state immediately.
* **Directional Reset:** You missed that if you have two drops in a row (), the second drop isn't just a "fail"; it is actually the **start** of a new valid sequence 
(length 2), not a full reset to 1.







1191. K-Concatenation Maximum Sum                                  B 
### Key Logic

* **Case :** Standard Kadane’s on a single array.
* **Case :** The maximum subarray can either stay within one array, span across the boundary of two arrays, or include the entire middle  arrays.
* **Total Sum Impact:** If the sum of the single array is positive, every additional full copy of the array increases the potential maximum sum.
* **Two-Pass Kadane:** Running Kadane on two concatenated copies () captures the maximum possible sum that wraps around the boundary. If  and the total sum is positive, 
we simply add the remaining  full array sums to this result.
